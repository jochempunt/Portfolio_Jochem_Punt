Bachelor-Thesis: Development of an AR product configurator and the analysis of the AI utility value. (In cooperation with CAS Software AG)

Nowadays, companies enable customers to customize their products using product configuration software. Especially for complex products such as cars, the visualization of the result is crucial. Augmented Reality (AR) offers potential here, but is limited by complex user interfaces. My work therefore focuses on the development of an AR product configurator with an integrated, voice-controlled AI assistant. The assistant is based on a Large Language Model (LLM) to process natural language. The core idea lies in the synergy between augmented reality and artificial intelligence (AI). Based on this, this thesis presents an innovative solution that not only simplifies product configuration, but also optimizes usability by combining both technologies.

(developed with Unity, ARFoundation, and GPT4)

Game Design Prototype: Temporal Transit Tales / DER BAHNHOF

is a 3D adventure mystery game set in a mysterious train station where time seems to stand still. The player takes on the role of a reporter named Jack, who is equipped with a special gadget - a time control machine and also a camera. The gameplay of the game is a combination of exploration, puzzle solving and detective work. The player will be able to fast forward or rewind time by 15 seconds in a limited area, to reveal clues and events that will help solve the puzzles and uncover grievances in the station. The game is set in a dystopian steampunk setting of the 1910s. The aesthetics and atmosphere of the station will provide players with a captivating backdrop, rich in detail and mystery.

I was responsible for the development of the prototype (with Unity) and the implementation of the game sound (with WWise) in this project.

Interactive Audio Study: Desert Drift

Modern racing games (like: Forza Horizon, Gran Tourismo, Need for Speed) often have linear soundtracks or use conventional pop music. With this design study I wanted to find out how to make music in racing games more interactive and and reactive.
I used Unity and Wwise for the implementation. For the starting point I used a freely available racing game project, which contained 2 tracks and a control system for cars.
I also produced all the music in this game

Blender Addon: Planet-Generator
